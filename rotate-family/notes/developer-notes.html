Current thinking is that this is a two-day lesson, with the first four activities on day 1 and the remaining activities on day 2. The Go Further activity is optional.

After the HFWA field test I've moved away from the idea of students keeping everything in an online journal, for two reasons. (a) If each pair has only one computer, it's distracting and awkward to go back and forth between journal and sketch. (b) There's considerable value in students copying on paper the traces they've created on the screen. Just as dragging x around with the mouse is useful, so is the process of using a pencil on paper to show where they dragged x and where x' moved as a result.

The current idea is to have a single-page worksheet for each day, with a significant amount of students' work to be recorded on the back (blank side) of the paper.

The part 1 worksheet (activities 1 to 4) is linked from activity 1, and the part 2 worksheet is linked from activity 5. The teacher notes need to explain all this.

Trace a Star

This simple three-page sketch with no tools introduces the student to the techniques needed to construct stars. The videos also show two methods, one with the center of rotation at the center of the star and the other to show that there's a different way to do it, with the center of rotation midway between two of the points of the star.

This sketch now uses the penguin and frog.

Rotate Dances

This is now simpler, asking students to do a 180° dance and a 90° dance with a simple pattern (moving only radially and circularly) and two more dances using movements that are less intuitive for the dependent variable. The penguin and frog appear in a sketch with sample choreography for all four dances.

It would be best to have an actual student video showing one of the sample dance patterns.
